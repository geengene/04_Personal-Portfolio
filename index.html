I'm

It's the year 2023, when my coding journey took its nascent footsteps in the confines of my NS bunk. The endless boundaries of what code can achieve simultaneously induced the nescient child in me who had discovered math contained alphabets, but also the inquisitive caveman who had just discovered fire. I wanted to masterfully wield this fire to breathe life to my ideas. I am interested in all things computing: ethical hacking, game development, web/app development, embedded systems, theoretical computing, and many more concepts where one lifetime is simply to fleeting to learn it all.

Currently, I am building a stronger understanding in full stack web development, as it brings into fruition a lot of the project ideas I am planning to build. I believe the Web is what brings ideas to fruition, across the globe in a visually accessible manner, to the fingertips of anyone who has a computer. Looking ahead, I plan to dabble into some IOT projects, brush up on my theoretical knowledge on *Data Structures and Algorithms* so I can crush more leetcode easy questions, as well some game dev and machine learning.

When I'm not just a cadaver punching gobbledygook code into my computer, I spend my unemployed time(hire me) playing guitar, producing Singapore's next hit single, working out, living an alternate life in open world games, reading, and contemplating life's eternal dread and the fleeting passage of time.

I'm

I had started up a Programming with Mosh (https://www.youtube.com/@programmingwithmosh) tutorial on python, learning about the quintessential programming concepts like data structures and OOP. During which, I was consuming programming content like there was no tomorrow. From watching [Michael Reeves](https://www.youtube.com/@MichaelReeves) building chaotically ridiculous tech to dystopian/historical films related to computing(Imitation Game, HER etc.), the possibilities of what code can do excited me and my hands were itching to write. I remember vividly building my very first snake game with turtle graphics in python. It baffled me that such a deceptively simple game had such complex mechanics. Tying in the scoring system, the snake growth, game speed, and end game logic, was like painting a piece of art. I then dabbled into the world of competitive programming solving problems on Codewars and Leetcode, which challenged my computational, problem solving and logical skills(hair strands were pulled). Not before getting lost in the endless sea of content surrounding computer science, unsure of what to learn or where to begin.

Juggling between rigorous NS training and my other hobbies, between pockets of time, I had begun learning full stack web development, along with building side projects like a trading bot in python, building my own API with FastAPI, diving deeper into [[Data Structures and Algorithms]] as well as spending days configuring developer environment tools like Neovim, terminal multiplexers, shell enhancements, all to chase the perfect setup(not a complete waste of time, I got pretty good at reading documentation).
