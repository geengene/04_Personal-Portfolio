HELLO,

I'm Gene

It's the year 2023, when my coding journey took its nascent footsteps in the confines of my NS bunk. I had started up a [Programming with Mosh](https://www.youtube.com/@programmingwithmosh) tutorial on python, learning about the quintessential programming concepts like data structures and OOP. During which, i was consuming programming content like there was no tomorrow. From watching [Michael Reeves](https://www.youtube.com/@MichaelReeves) building chaotically ridiculous tech to dystopian/historical films related to computing(imitation game, HER etc.), and the possibilities of what code can do excited me and my hands were itching to write, or rather, type out my own journey, and i wasted no time, building simple terminal apps, followed by building GUI applications that taught me a lot about clean code and how organization ties in with logic. I remember vividly building my very first snake game with turtle graphics in python, which at that point of time baffled me with the complexity behind what i thought to be a simple game. I then dabbled into the world of competitive programming solving problems on Codewars and Leetcode, which challenged my computational, problem solving and logical skills(hair strands were pulled). But of course, typical of one learning a new skill, not before getting lost in the endless sea of content surrounding computer science, unsure of what to learn or where to begin. Currently, I am building a stronger foundation in full stack web development, as it aligns with bringing to fruition a lot of the project ideas i had planned to build and also that the Web is what brings ideas across the globe to be accessible to the fingertips of anyone who has a computer in a visual manner.
